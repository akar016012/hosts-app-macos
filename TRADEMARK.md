# Trademark & Brand Policy

The **AGPLv3 license covers the *code*. It does *not* grant any rights to the
project's *name, logo, or branding*.** Trademark is a separate, independent
protection: even though anyone may use, modify, and redistribute the source under
AGPLv3, they may **not** use the project's brand in a way that confuses users or
implies endorsement. This is the lever that stops a competitor from shipping a
fork *under your name*.

> Not legal advice. To get enforceable trademark rights, consult an IP attorney
> and consider registering the mark (e.g. with the USPTO).

## ⚠️ Important: pick a distinctive brand name

The current app name, **"Hosts,"** is almost certainly **too generic/descriptive
to register or enforce** as a trademark — it plainly describes a hosts-file
editor, and descriptive terms get little to no trademark protection. The repo
name `hosts-app-macos` has the same problem.

**To actually have a defensible brand, adopt a distinctive (ideally coined) name**
— something arbitrary or invented that doesn't describe the product, e.g. a made-up
word or an unrelated word used suggestively. A distinctive name + a distinctive
logo are what you register and enforce. Until you choose one, the sections below
describe the *policy*; the *strength* of the mark depends on that choice.

Replace `<BRAND>` below with the distinctive name once chosen.
