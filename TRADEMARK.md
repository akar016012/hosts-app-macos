# Trademark & Brand Policy

The **AGPLv3 license covers the *code*. It does *not* grant any rights to the
project's *name, logo, or branding*.** Trademark is a separate, independent
protection: even though anyone may use, modify, and redistribute the source under
AGPLv3, they may **not** use the project's brand in a way that confuses users or
implies endorsement. This is the lever that stops a competitor from shipping a
fork *under your name*.

> Not legal advice. To get enforceable trademark rights, consult an IP attorney
> and consider registering the mark (e.g. with the USPTO).

## ⚠️ Note on brand distinctiveness

The app name **"Hosts"** is likely **too generic/descriptive to register or
enforce** as a trademark — it plainly describes a hosts-file editor, and
descriptive terms receive little to no trademark protection under US law. If you
wish to obtain enforceable trademark rights, consider adopting a coined or
distinctive name and registering it (e.g. with the USPTO). Until then, the
policy below still governs use of the current name and logo.

## What you may do without permission

- State **truthfully** that your product is "built on Hosts," "a fork of
  Hosts," or "compatible with Hosts," using the name **nominatively** —
  i.e. only as much as needed to identify the original, without implying
  endorsement.
- Use the name in articles, tutorials, reviews, and discussion.
- Redistribute **unmodified** official builds under the project name.

## What you may NOT do without written permission

- Ship a **modified version / fork under the name Hosts or a confusingly
  similar name**, or use the Hosts icon as your product's icon. Fork the code
  freely (AGPLv3 allows it) — but **rename your fork** and use your own icon.
- Use the Marks in your company name, product name, domain name, app-store
  listing title, or social handles in a way that suggests it is the official
  project or that the Maintainer endorses it.
- Modify the Marks, or combine them with your own marks, in a way that could
  cause confusion.

## Forks

You are encouraged to fork — that's the point of open source. When you do:

1. Choose a **different name and icon** for your fork.
2. You may note "based on Hosts" truthfully (see above).
3. Don't present your fork as the official Hosts project.
